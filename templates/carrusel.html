Beauty Makeup Course

In this class, makeup artists practice beauty and corrective cosmetic applications for eyes, lips, cheeks and skin. They also learn about how to take care of the skin before applying any make up to the face. Makeup courses include learning the appropriate makeup looks for every day, and special occasions. Students enrolled in makeup courses use foundation, powder, blush, eyeshadow, mascara, eyeliner, lip pencil and lipstick. Classroom demonstration learning focuses on face shapes and skin tones, color theory, application styles and cosmetic trends.




Beginning Garment Sewing

This class is for those students who wish to simply learn how to use their sewing machine. Students will make either pajama shorts or pants for their first project. Then, they may select any project they choose. This class covers the fundamentals of beginning sewing for the new sewer, “haven’t-sewn-for-years” sewer, and those who desire a refresher. Students choose their own project and work at their own speed. Includes measuring for pattern size, pattern selection and pattern envelope reading, fabric and notion selection, pattern layout, cutting, sewing machine use, and sewing to complete the garment.




Hairstyling Course

After completing a basic beauty makeup course, students often enroll in a basic hairstyling class. This course covers a full complement of styling tools, like blow dryers, hot rollers and irons, and their uses for preparing period and contemporary hairstyles. Students practice general hairstyling methods and learn which styles best compliment the model or client according to face shape and occasion.




Bakery Classes

This course is a study of the fundamentals of baking including, dough, quick breads, pies, cakes, cookies, tarts and basic items made in a bakery. Topics include baking terminology, tool and equipment use, formula conversions, functions of ingredients, and the use of proper flours.

Plaza Comunitaria

Madres de Casino Rd is pleased to provide our community a Plaza Comunitaria in Snohomish County, Washington. The objective of La Plaza Comunitaria is to encourage, promote and operate educational services for Spanish speaking adults, who live, in our case in Snohomish County. This program educates people who are illiterate and people who only have a few years of school. By providing this program to our community, we are preparing people to start the GED program in any Community College.

In the past four years, we have been preparing people from our community to take the GED. We have provided tutoring to more than 40 students, which 35 out of them have obtained their diploma. With this program we have seen dramatic improvements with our students; they feel more confident and many of them have decided to continue their education in the local Community College; some have become nurse assistant, others hair dressers and more.

We have seen measurable success and now we are seeking to expand our education program by opening a Plaza Comunitaria. This work program in terms of adult education is presented between the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Mexico (Secretaría de Relaciones Exteriores - SRE), the National Institute for Adult Education (Instituto Nacional para la Educación de los Adultos - INEA) and Madres de Casino Rd. The INEA only provides the program and Madres de Casino Rd must provide the classroom, computers, books, full furnished office, a full-time employee and of course the possibilities to the students to make photocopies. As you can tell, this is a wonderful program (project) but, it requires us to have funding to sustain it.

At the beginning, our Plaza Comunitaria will be offered 2 days per week during diverse hours, so people can find the best time slot for their convenience. Our goal is to open 5 days per week, but that will depend on the demand.

Besides the place where people can go study, a computer for testing, books, and photocopies, we are offering tutors so people can come and ask whatever question they might have. We will have a math and a Spanish tutor; each will work 10 hrs. per week.
